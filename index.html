Below is a template of protective clauses you might include in a booking agreement. These are designed to safeguard your interests during the ongoing partition process. While this provides a starting point, having it reviewed by an independent legal professional would be ideal if possible.

Essential Protective Clauses

1. Conditional Nature of Agreement
"This booking agreement is expressly conditional upon the successful completion of the ongoing land partition (taqseem) process for Khewat No. [number] in [village/location]. 2. Timeline Protection
"While both parties acknowledge the intent to complete this transaction in a timely manner, no specific timeline for completion is guaranteed due to the unpredictable nature of the partition proceedings. This agreement shall remain valid for a period of [X months] from signing, with automatic extensions of [Y months] unless either party provides written notice of termination."
3. Booking Amount and Refund Terms
"The booking amount of Rs. [amount] represents [X%] of the full purchase price of Rs. [full amount]. This booking amount is refundable in full to the Buyer under the following circumstances only:
  • If the land partition process is abandoned or fails to complete within [time period]
  • If government restrictions prevent the transfer after partition
  • If the Seller chooses not to sell after partition for any reason, in which case a penalty of [small percentage] shall be added to the refund
In all other circumstances, should the Buyer choose to withdraw from this agreement, the booking amount shall be forfeited to the Seller as liquidated damages."
4. Price Protection
"The full purchase price is hereby fixed at Rs. [amount] per acre for a total of Rs. [total amount] for the entire parcel. This price is non-negotiable and shall not be subject to reduction based on delays in the partition process, changes in market conditions, or representations made by any third parties including but not limited to legal representatives of either party."
5. Direct Communication Clause
"Both parties agree to communicate directly with each other regarding all substantive matters related to this transaction. While legal representatives may facilitate the process, no binding modifications to this agreement may be made through third parties without the express written consent of both Buyer and Seller."
6. Confidentiality Provision
"The existence, terms, and conditions of this booking agreement shall remain strictly confidential between the parties until the successful completion of the land partition process. Neither party shall disclose this agreement to any third parties except as required by law or to their direct legal counsel who shall be bound by the same confidentiality obligations. Any breach of this confidentiality provision by either party shall be grounds for termination of this agreement at the non-breaching party's discretion."
7. Non-Interference Clause
"Both parties agree not to interfere with or attempt to influence the land partition process except through proper legal channels. The Buyer shall not contact or attempt to influence any officials, legal representatives, or other parties involved in the partition process without the Seller's express written permission."
8. Representation Acknowledgment
"The Seller acknowledges that they have engaged [Advocate Name] for the limited purpose of facilitating the land partition process. Both parties acknowledge that said advocate does not represent either party in this transaction and has no authority to negotiate, modify, or interpret this booking agreement."
9. Documentation of Land Condition
"The land is being booked in its present condition and location. A detailed land description including boundaries, access routes, and current usage is attached as Annexure A. Both parties have inspected the property and agree that this represents the accurate condition of the land at the time of booking."
10. Dispute Resolution
"Any disputes arising from this agreement shall be resolved through arbitration in [neutral location outside your immediate district] under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996. Both parties agree not to file civil suits related to this agreement without first attempting arbitration."

Using This Agreement
