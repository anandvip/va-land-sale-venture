Letter to Myself
16 April — Day of Taqseem

Dear Vipul,

Today is not just a date on the calendar. It’s a milestone of legacy, clarity, and courage.

This land — held for 32 years, nurtured through seasons of sun, sweat, and solitude — now stands at the brink of a new beginning. The hands that held it with love and responsibility are not letting go, they are simply passing the baton forward with dignity.

Let this be the day when shadows fall behind me.

I have done everything within my means:
– Walked the path of truth,
– Observed without illusion,
– Protected my family’s legacy,
– Listened to both intuition and facts,
– Trusted — but never blindly.

I stand today with my eyes wide open. I do not fear the outcome, for I know this:
What is mine by dharma shall remain mine by law.

The signatures served, the summons issued — this is not chaos, it is clarity taking form.
This is my land, my right, and my future — and no network, no manipulation, no hidden agenda can take away what I stand for.

If obstacles come, I will meet them with strategy, not stress.
If truth is delayed, I will wait, not waver.
If I’m tested, I will respond — not react.

From this day onward, I am not just exiting farming.
I am graduating with grace — from a life lived in service of land, to a life that now serves me and those I care for.

This land will carry my blessings.
This chapter will carry my name — not in conflict, but in closure.

So today, Vipul —
Breathe. Stand tall. And say it out loud if needed:

“I am ready to receive what is rightfully mine.
Let truth be my document, and dignity my signature.”

With full clarity,
— Vipul (the one who chooses peace with power)
